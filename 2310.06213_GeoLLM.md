# Literature Review: GeoLLM (ICLR 2024)

## 1. Research Question
[cite_start]The main goal of this research was to find out if the vast amount of knowledge "compressed" inside Large Language Models (LLMs) during their pre-training can be used for geospatial prediction tasks[cite: 13, 61]. [cite_start]The authors specifically wanted to see if LLMs could predict indicators like population density, asset wealth, or household income as effectively as traditional methods like satellite imagery[cite: 16, 23].

## 2. Methodology: How They Tackled the Problem
[cite_start]The researchers found that **zero-shot prompting** (simply asking the model a question without training it first) is not effective if you only use geographic coordinates[cite: 14, 127]. [cite_start]To solve this, they developed the **GeoLLM** method[cite: 15, 66].

### A. The "Smart" Prompt Design
[cite_start]Instead of using only latitude and longitude, they utilized **OpenStreetMap (OSM)** data to create a detailed text-based prompt[cite: 66, 131]:
* [cite_start]**Reverse-Geocoding:** They used the *Nominatim* tool to turn coordinates into a full address (neighborhood, city, county, and country)[cite: 132, 136].
* [cite_start]**Nearby Places:** They used the *Overpass API* to identify the 10 closest locations within a 100km radius[cite: 133, 136]. [cite_start]For each place, the prompt included its name, distance, and cardinal direction (e.g., "0.7 km North: Columbus Circle")[cite: 133, 562].
* [cite_start]**Task Context:** The prompt ended with a clear task name and a scale for the prediction (e.g., "0.0 to 9.9")[cite: 116, 556].

### B. Fine-Tuning the Models
[cite_start]The team didn't just design a prompt; they fine-tuned the models to connect their internal weights to these specific tasks[cite: 88, 102].
* [cite_start]**Models Used:** They compared GPT-3.5, Llama 2 (7B), and RoBERTa[cite: 17, 70].
* [cite_start]**The Process:** For Llama 2, they used **QLoRA** (4-bit quantization) to make the training efficient, training only 33 million parameters[cite: 155, 296].
* [cite_start]**Text-to-Prediction:** They treated the task as a classification problem rather than regression[cite: 121]. [cite_start]They converted numerical labels into 100 "bins" (0.0 to 9.9) so the model could "write" the answer as text[cite: 121, 124, 125].

## 3. Key Achievements and Results
* [cite_start]**Performance Boost:** The GeoLLM method showed a **70% improvement** in accuracy (measured by Pearson's $r^2$) compared to basic baselines[cite: 17, 313].
* [cite_start]**Outperforming Satellites:** GPT-3.5 often equaled or exceeded the performance of satellite-based benchmarks for predicting wealth and population[cite: 17, 289].
* [cite_start]**Sample Efficiency:** The models were very efficient, meaning they could make good predictions even with small amounts of training data (as few as 100 samples)[cite: 17, 266].
* [cite_start]**Global Robustness:** The method worked consistently well across different continents and was not limited to just one region[cite: 17, 277].

## 4. Conclusions
[cite_start]The authors concluded that LLMs are effectively **geospatial foundation models**[cite: 304]. [cite_start]They contain a "hidden map" of the world that can be unlocked with the right prompting strategy[cite: 76, 77]. [cite_start]This research is important because it offers a new, cheaper way to gather social and economic data in areas where satellite imagery might be too expensive or lack predictive power[cite: 18, 72].
